# Motion

**From motion to resolution.**

Motion is a committee management platform designed for Model United Nations conferences.

Built by delegates. Designed for chairs.

---

## Why Motion Exists

Most MUN committees rely on a collection of disconnected tools:

* Spreadsheets for attendance
* Timers for speeches
* Documents for resolutions
* Manual vote counting
* Multiple browser tabs

Motion aims to reduce administrative friction by bringing these workflows into a single platform while preserving traditional MUN procedure.

The goal is not to change MUN.

The goal is to make running MUN easier.

---

## Vision

Motion follows the lifecycle of a committee session:

```text
Motion → Formation → Debate → Resolution
```

Every feature should support one of these stages.

The software should support committee procedure, not replace it.

---

## Brand Identity

### Name

The name **Motion** originates from one of the most fundamental actions within MUN.

A motion starts discussion, guides committee procedure, and moves debate forward.

Motion represents progress.

### Tagline

**From motion to resolution.**

A committee begins with motions and concludes with resolutions.

The tagline reflects both the workflow of MUN and the purpose of the platform.

### Logo

The Motion logo consists of:

* A circle representing a motion in progress
* Four horizontal bars representing the stages of committee development

```text
Motion
Formation
Debate
Resolution
```

Together they symbolize movement, progression, and structured debate.

### Colors

#### Black & White

The primary palette represents:

* Professionalism
* Diplomacy
* Structure
* Neutrality
* Contrast

MUN is built around opposing viewpoints, debate, and negotiation. The black-and-white palette reflects that contrast while maintaining a formal appearance.

#### Brown Accent

The accent color represents:

* Warmth
* Community
* Approachability
* The human side of MUN

While MUN is professional, it is also a community built by students who genuinely enjoy debate, diplomacy, and international affairs.

Motion aims to reflect both sides of that experience.
